Superstore Dataset
📌 Business Problem
How can a retail superstore grow revenue, identify its most valuable customers, and improve operational efficiency across regions, product categories, and shipping methods?
This project analyzes 4 years of sales data (2015–2018) to answer that question using Excel, SQL, and Power BI.

🔍 Key Findings
💰 Revenue Overview

$2,261,537 total revenue across 4,922 orders from 793 unique customers (2015–2018)
Average order value: $459

🌍 Regional Performance

West region leads in total revenue at $710,219
East region has the highest average order value at $489/order — stronger per-transaction efficiency despite lower volume

📦 Category Insights

Technology is the top category at 36.59% of revenue ($827,455)
Revenue is fairly balanced: Furniture (32.22%) and Office Supplies (31.19%) follow closely

👥 Customer Segments

Consumer segment drives the most revenue at $1,148,060
Corporate and Home Office customers spend more per order ($462 and $475), representing higher individual value

📈 Growth Trend

Revenue dipped 4.26% in 2016, then recovered with 30.64% growth in 2017 and 20.30% growth in 2018 — a strong business turnaround

🏆 Top Customers

Sean Miller is the highest-value customer at $25,045 across just 2 orders — highlighting key account concentration risk

🚚 Shipping Efficiency

Standard Class dominates with 2,945 orders but averages 5 days to ship
Same Day shipping is severely underutilized at only 261 orders — a potential revenue and customer satisfaction opportunity


💡 Business Recommendations

Invest in the West region while studying East's efficiency model to replicate high order values elsewhere
Protect key accounts like Sean Miller with loyalty or account management strategies
Promote Same Day shipping — low adoption suggests a marketing or awareness gap
Double down on Technology products which drive the highest revenue share
